Impact of Social Media Sentiment on Stock



Audience, Data & Topics

Audience

The target audience includes both seasoned and junior equity investors with a basic understanding of the stock market and trading. They are interested in uncovering insights and relationships between social media sentiments and stock returns and volatility.

Data

Our dataset from Kaggle includes columns such as tweet, stock, date, last_price, and various return periods. Data cleaning involved delimiting columns, removing invalid tweets, ensuring proper encoding, and handling special characters within text fields to maintain data integrity.

First, let's explore the impact of news sentiment on future stock returns

  • This chart provides a comprehensive analysis of the relationship between social media sentiment and future stock returns. It assists investors in understanding how varying levels of sentiment influence stock returns over different time horizons.
  • Stock Level Chart on top display the close prices and average sentiment scores over time for selected stocks. These charts provide a comprehensive view of the market performance and investor sentiment, allowing for a better understanding of the correlation between stock prices and market sentiment. By analyzing these trends, stakeholders can make informed decisions based on both historical price movements and prevailing market attitudes.
  • Two Bar Charts visualizes the return trajectory across different sentiment quantiles, covering various periods: 1-day, 2-day, 3-day, and 7-day returns. Tweet sentiments are split into four quartiles, with strong negative, slightly negative, \ slightly positive and strong positive.
  • Four Scatter Plots illustrates the correlation between sentiment polarity and future stock returns for different periods. Each scatter plot includes a regression line to show the trend. The color and size of the dots represent the correlation strength.
  • Insight:
    • Significant downturn on 7 days return for very negative sentiments
    • Very positive sentiment leads to consistent price increase
    • The pattern becomes more significant over longer time horizons

Second, let’s see the relationship between tweet word count and future stock returns

Use this chart to see how the word count of tweets with positive and negative sentiment correlates with future stock returns. By examining these relationships, you can gain insights into the tweets and its potential impact on stock price movements.

How to Use the Chart

Stock Filter: Use the dropdown menu to select specific stocks you want to analyze. This allows you to focus on individual stocks and see how tweet word counts relate to their future returns.

Data Points: Each point on the scatterplot represents the average word count of tweets for a particular stock on a given day and the corresponding future return.

Regression Lines: The blue lines indicate the trend of the data points, showing the general relationship between tweet word count and future returns.

Understanding the Chart

Positive Sentiment vs. Future Returns: The left column of charts shows the relationship between tweet word counts with positive sentiment and future returns over different time frames (1-day, 2-day, 3-day, and 7-day).

Negative Sentiment vs. Future Returns: The right column of charts displays the same relationship but for tweets with negative sentiment.

Key Insights

Trend Analysis: By comparing the charts, you can observe how the sentiment of tweets (positive or negative) and their word counts influence future stock returns.

Time Frame Comparison: Analyze how the impact of tweet word count varies over different durations. This can help in understanding short-term versus longer-term effects.

Sentiment Impact: See how positive and negative sentiments differ in their influence on future returns, providing a comprehensive view of market sentiment and stock performance.

How does the volume of tweets correlate with stock volatility?

  • Next, we'll explore the correlation between tweet volume and stock volatility. Use the left chart to view stock volatility rankings and click on individual stocks to see how their tweet volume correlates with volatility. Stocks with insufficient data (fewer than 5 days of data) have been excluded. Here’s additional context for the charts and insights you can explore:

  • Volatility: Indicates how much a stock’s price fluctuates over a given period, reflecting the risk associated with the stock. 10-Day Volatility: Measures the standard deviation of returns over the past 10 trading days, capturing short-term fluctuations. 30-Day Volatility: Measures the standard deviation of returns over the past 30 trading days, reflecting longer-term trends.

  • Tweet Count: Represents the number of tweets for each stock on each date.

  • Scatter Plot(correlation chart on the right): X-Axis: Tweet count for a given day. Y-Axis: Stock volatility following the tweet date. Points: Each represents tweet count and corresponding volatility for that day. Red Lines: Regression lines indicating the trend and general relationship between tweet volume and stock volatility.

  • Insights: Identify stocks with higher volatility following tweets. Discover stocks with a positive correlation between tweet count and volatility. Find stocks with a negative correlation between tweet count and volatility.
